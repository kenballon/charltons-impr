Introduction

As of 23 May 2025, Hong Kong has introduced a new corporate re-domiciliation regime under the Companies (Amendment) (No. 2) Ordinance 2023, now incorporated into Part 17A of the Companies Ordinance (Cap. 622) (the “CO”). This development permits eligible non-Hong Kong incorporated entities to transfer their place of incorporation to Hong Kong and continue as companies incorporated under Hong Kong law. What is Company Re-domiciliation?

Re-domiciliation enables a foreign company to change its place of incorporation to Hong Kong without the need to wind up in its original jurisdiction or establish a new Hong Kong company from scratch. Once registered in Hong Kong under section 820C of the CO, the re- domiciled entity is treated in law as a Hong Kong-incorporated company, subject to the rights, obligations, and compliance requirements under the CO, unless otherwise specified.

Key Eligibility Criteria

To qualify for re-domiciliation to Hong Kong, a company must, among other things:

  • Be permitted under its existing jurisdiction’s laws to transfer its domicile and comply with the relevant local requirements;
  • Be of a substantially similar company type as those recognised under the CO;
  • Have completed at least one financial year since incorporation;
  • Satisfy solvency and good faith requirements;
  • Ensure the application is not intended to defraud creditors or contravene public interest.

Eligible company types for re-domiciliation include private or public companies limited by shares, as well as unlimited companies with share capital.

Application Procedure

Applicants must submit to the Registrar of Companies:

  • A completed Re-domiciliation Form (Form NNC6);​
  • A certified copy of the proposed articles of association;​
  • A legal opinion from counsel qualified in the original jurisdiction;
  • Supporting constitutional, financial, and compliance documents; and​
  • A Notice to the Business Registration Office (Form IRBR5).

Applications may be filed either electronically or in hard copy. A Certificate of Re- domiciliation and Business Registration Certificate will be issued upon successful registration.

Post-Registration Obligations

Following re-domiciliation, the company must:​

  • Effect deregistration in its original jurisdiction within 120 days;​
  • Comply with all statutory filing requirements under Hong Kong law, including filings relating to directors, members, and charges; and​
  • Maintain ongoing compliance as required of all companies incorporated in Hong Kong.
